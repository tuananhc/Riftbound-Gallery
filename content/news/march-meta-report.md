Two weeks in, and the Set 3 meta has taken shape faster than any previous set. Here's what the data says.

## Tier 1: The Dominant Strategies

**Fire/Shadow Aggro** sits comfortably at the top of the ladder. The combination of early burn spells and the new *Echo* mechanic on shadow recursion tools creates a tempo advantage that most midrange decks can't recover from. Expect to face this on the ladder.

**Arcane Control** is the other powerhouse — slower, but oppressive once it stabilises. The Rift Conduit reprint in Set 3 gave the archetype the mana fixing it lacked in Set 2.

## Tier 2: Competitive but Counterable

**Earth/Light Midrange** has surprised everyone. The Stoneweave Ward + Rootbound Colossus shell is legitimately powerful, and the addition of the new *Terraform* Enchantment cycle gives it real late-game reach.

**Water Tempo** remains consistent. Tidecaller Sage continues to be one of the best value creatures in the format.

## What to Play at Spring Invitational

If you're preparing for the Spring Invitational next month, Fire/Shadow is the safest choice. However, if you expect a heavy field of Fire/Shadow mirrors, Earth/Light is well-positioned to prey on it.

*Meta data sourced from 10,000 ranked ladder games across Platinum–Diamond brackets, March 1–13.*
